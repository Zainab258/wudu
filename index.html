What is Wudhu?

Wudhu, also known as ablution is a ritual purification process in Islam. It involves cleansing certain parts of the body before engaging
in acts of worship such as prayer. Wudhu symbolises spiritual cleanliness and readiness to stand before Allah.

How to make Wudhu

To perform Wudhu, follow these this basic steps:

  • Begin with the intention(niyyah) to perform wudhu for the purpose of purification
  • Wash your hands up to the wrists three times.
  • Rinse your mouth and sniff water into your nostrils, each three times.
  • Wash your face three times, from the hairline to the chin and from ear to ear.
  • Wash your arms up to the elbows three times, starting with the right arm.
  • Wipe your head once, moving from the front to the back and back to the front.
  • Wipe the inside and outside of both ears with bwet index fingers and thumbs.
  • Wash your feet up to trhe ankles three times, startingf with the right ankle foot.

PERFORMING SALAH

Now that you've performed wudu, you're ready to perform salah. Salah consists of various physical and verbal acts of worship performed in a specific sequence.It includes recitation from the holy Quran, standing, bowing, postrating, and sitting in a prescribed manner.

  • Fajr: Dawn prayer.
  • Duhr: Noon prayer
  • Asr: Afternoon prayer
  • Maghrib: Evening Prayer
  • Isha: Night prayer

Each prayer consists of a set of number of Ra'kah, with specific recitations and positions.
